#  Financial Sales Analysis Report (2013–2014)

##  Project Overview

This Power BI dashboard delivers a comprehensive analysis of **sales performance** across multiple segments, products, and geographies for the years **2013–2014**. The goal is to derive actionable insights that can help stakeholders improve **profitability, discount strategy**, and **sales focus** across markets.

The report simulates the work of a **Sales or Business Analyst** in the FMCG or retail sector, delivering insights around total sales, product profitability, discounts, and units sold across regions.

---

##  Objectives

- Analyze gross sales, net sales, and profit across product segments
- Understand how discounts affect product profitability
- Identify top-performing products by sales and profit
- Evaluate sales and unit distribution by geography
- Offer actionable insights to improve pricing and marketing strategy

---

##  Dataset Overview

**Source:** Simulated FMCG sales dataset  
**Time Period:** 2013–2014

- `Gross Sales` – $128M

##  Dashboard Visuals & Insights

### 1.  Sales by Segment

- **Top segment**: $53M  
- Followed by: $42M and $20M segments  
- Lowest-performing segments generated just $2M, indicating low-impact or niche markets.

### 2.  Units Sold by Product

- Highest selling product sold **0.17M units**, while others are closely competitive (~0.15–0.16M)
- Product volumes are relatively evenly distributed

### 3.  Discounts by Product

- **Velo** accounts for the largest discount share: **3M (28.3%)**
- **Amarilla** and **Montana** also receive significant discounts (17.1% and 15.8% respectively)
- Discount structure may require rebalancing for margin improvement

### 4.  Profit by Product

- **Paseo** leads with **$5M (28.4%)** in total profit
- Other contributors: Carretera ($3M), Montana and VTT ($2M each)
- Discount-heavy products (e.g., Velo) return lower profit

### 5.  Units Sold by Country

- Geospatial view highlights concentrated markets in **North America and Europe**
- Could inform expansion or resource allocation decisions

### 6.  Gross Sales Trend by Segment

- Visual shows descending revenue trend among segments:
- $56M → $46M → $21M → $2M
- Clear revenue drop between top 3 and remaining segments

---

##  Business Insights

1. **Margin Optimization Needed**  
   - Products like **Velo** receive high discounts but don't contribute equally to profit. Recommend reviewing discount-to-margin ratios.

2. **Sales Focus Should Shift to High-Profit Products**  
   - **Paseo and Carretera** offer strong profitability. Consider prioritizing them in campaigns and supply chain investments.

3. **Segment-Level Performance Disparity**  
   - Top 3 segments dominate sales. Bottom segments may require targeted promotions, rebranding, or phase-out planning.

---

##  Strategic Recommendations

| Area                | Recommendation                                                  |
|---------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Discount Policy   | Reduce unnecessary discounting on low-profit products           |
| Product Strategy  | Focus on profitable products like Paseo and Carretera           |
| Market Expansion  | Use country-level insights to inform sales resource planning    |
| Inventory         | Align inventory with high-selling SKUs for demand fulfillment   |
| Reporting         | Adopt this dashboard as a recurring monthly sales health check  |
